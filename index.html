Blog

Research indicates that 1 in every 8 infants is sexually abused, 2 in every 6 neglected, 2 in every 7 emotionally abused, 21% physically abused by maids in Uganda between 2014 and 2018. In the recent 5 years, two particular nanny incidents in Uganda and Nigeria went viral on the internet and continue to shock Africa and the world. In this section, I’ll share short videos and summaries of these events which have exposed the ugly side of the nanny problem and the urgent need to tackle this issue that is widespread across Africa.

Two particular cases detailed below are one of those that shocked the World (Events That Reveal The Ugly Nature Of The Nanny Problem Across Africa):

Uganda – Jolly Tumuhiirwe, the vicious nanny

Sometime last year (2014), Jolly Tumuhiirwe, a nanny in a Ugandan household was caught on a hidden camera as she viciously beat, kicked and stomped on the 18-month old toddler she was hired to take care of. The video of this brutality (shown below) was shared by the father of the child on social media and it quickly went viral in Uganda and many parts of Africa. This video tells a horrifying story that unfortunately happens everyday in many homes on the continent.

Nigeria – Funmilayo Adeyemi, The Kidnapper Nanny

In March 2015, Funmilayo, a newly hired nanny, kidnapped three kids, including an 11-month baby from their home in Lagos, Nigeria. For the next couple of days, the Orekoya household was severely traumatized as the kidnappers demanded a ransom of 13 million Naira (about $80,000) from the parents. In the video below, the mother, a banker and co-breadwinner of the home, agonises and is visibly broken by the sudden loss of her three children to kidnappers, through the deception of a nanny she barely knew.



About Us

Mom-ager is a community based app established to provide peace of mind for busy mothers who cannot be with their young children while at work so have to leave them with the maids at home.As a partnership, we have managed to thoroughly screen the nannies that we shall use and worked hand in hand with the kenyan police to ensure safety of the children assigned to us by their parents. Unlike other daycare services, mom-ager has both day and night services, provides transport facilities for the children whose parents will be interested in working with us so they do not have to get caught up in traffic jam dropping off and picking up their children. We also provide mobile nannies and those who work at the day-care centres.
